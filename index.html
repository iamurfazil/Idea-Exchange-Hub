Explore the following problems and think about possible solutions:

Lack of Quality Education

Many schools in rural areas lack basic infrastructure and qualified teachers. Many schools lack basic facilities like proper classrooms, sanitation, electricity, and clean drinking water. Insufficient learning materials such as textbooks, libraries, and computers.

Financial Constraints

Financial constraints can prevent students from continuing their education, leading to high dropout rates. This can have long-term impacts on their future prospects and the economy as a whole. The effects can include difficulty in meeting basic needs, delaying business operations, or compromising project quality.

Pressure of Competitive Exams

The intense focus on competitive exams causes stress among students.

Digital Divide

Many students, especially in rural areas, lack access to digital devices and the internet for online learning.

Unemployment After Graduation

Many graduates struggle to find jobs due to skill gaps and lack of industry-relevant training.

Mental Health Issues

Stigma and lack of mental health support make it difficult for students to seek help.
